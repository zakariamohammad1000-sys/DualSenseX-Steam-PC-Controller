Once the application opens, follow these steps to connect your device.

1. Connect your PS5 controller to your PC using a USB-C cable.
2. The application will detect the device immediately.
3. If you prefer a wireless connection, open your Windows Bluetooth settings.
4. Put your PS5 controller into pairing mode. Hold the Share and PS buttons until the light bar flashes.
5. Select "Wireless Controller" in the Windows Bluetooth menu.
6. The DualSenseX app will recognize the wireless connection automatically.

## 💻 Making the Controller Work with Steam

Steam manages controller inputs for most games. If you want the best results, adjust your Steam settings to prevent conflicts.

1. Open Steam.
2. Go to the Settings menu.
3. Click on the Controller tab.
4. Turn off "PlayStation Configuration Support." This prevents Steam from interfering with the advanced instructions sent by DualSenseX.
5. Launch your game through Steam or directly from your desktop.

## 🔍 Solving Common Issues

If you run into trouble, check these common fixes first.

- The application does not see your controller: Unplug the device and plug it back in. Ensure no other controller software is currently running.
- Low performance: Close web browsers or other heavy programs while playing.
- No vibration: Check that the driver for the device is active. Go to the Device Manager in Windows to confirm that the game controller entry shows no errors.
- Bluetooth drops: Move your PC away from other wireless interference or update your Bluetooth driver.
- Game does not detect the controller: Ensure you select the Virtual Controller output in the main dashboard of the app. This creates a fake Xbox controller that Windows understands easily.
